# Shifu Health — Business Context

> Source: [shifu.health](https://www.shifu.health) (scraped June 2026)  
> Entity: Shifu Health, a product of **Supamill Inc.**

## One-liner

**One App & Wellness Team** — Replace multiple wellness apps with a single app backed by nutrition and fitness experts who review plans, answer questions, and keep members on track.

## What Shifu Does

Shifu is an **AI-first wellness platform** that combines:

1. **Coach (AI)** — Handles day-to-day wellness: nutrition logging, training plans, reminders, macro tracking, grocery lists, wearable-informed plan adjustments, and conversational coaching over text/iMessage-style chat.
2. **Human experts (Health Team tier)** — Real coaches and nutritionists looped in when the moment calls for a human: plan reviews, escalations, form/programming changes, and accountability.
3. **Unified mobile app (iOS)** — Plan, logs, progress, and Health Team messaging in one place.

### Core product thesis

> **AI handles the day to day. Humans when you need them.**

Coach runs daily wellness autonomously but can **escalate to real coaches** in-thread (e.g., @Spencer, @Aaron) when complexity, injury risk, or member request warrants human judgment.

## Key Capabilities

| Capability | Description |
|---|---|
| **Conversational coaching** | Text-first interface; members don't need to open the app for every interaction |
| **Persistent memory** | Goals, schedule, habits, macros, meals, grocery lists, training history, mood, sleep, soreness, adherence, life events |
| **Adaptive planning** | Integrates **Oura, Whoop, Garmin**; adjusts workouts and meals based on recovery/sleep without member asking |
| **Nutrition automation** | Macro-aware meal suggestions, grocery list memory, DoorDash links to macro-fitting orders |
| **Human-in-the-loop** | Coaches review food logs, approve volume changes, deload mileage, escalate to nutritionists |
| **Instructional content** | Warm-up videos, exercise demos, structured cool-downs embedded in plans |

## Target Customers

Athletes, founders, students, and parents. Testimonials from engineers/founders at Google, Apple, Waymo, Y Combinator companies, Asana, etc.

### Member pain points Shifu solves

- Too many disconnected wellness apps
- Laziness/friction opening nutrition apps for every log
- Confusion at the gym without tailored daily workouts
- Diet decision fatigue ("big foodie" — wants healthier choices made for them)
- Need accountability without a physically present trainer
- Plans that don't adapt to schedule, recovery, or injury

## Business Model Signals

- **Waitlist** stage (pre-scale GTM)
- **Health Team tier** — paid tier with dedicated human experts
- **Coach roles** referenced in footer (product-led team structure)
- B2C wellness, not clinical diagnostics or FDA-regulated medical devices

## Technology & AI Positioning

- AI Coach trained on **evidence-based practice** by human wellness coaches
- **Wearable integrations** for passive data-driven adaptation
- **Multi-party chat threads** (member + AI + human coaches in one conversation)
- **Proactive outreach** (Coach initiates check-ins on meals, recovery, adherence)
- **Action execution** — updates plans, adds grocery items, sends DoorDash links, logs meals from photos/descriptions

## Explicit Boundaries (Important for Research Comparison)

| Shifu IS | Shifu IS NOT |
|---|---|
| General wellness & fitness coaching | Clinical diagnosis |
| Nutrition and exercise planning | Medical treatment plans |
| AI + human coaching for adherence | Autonomous diagnostic AI (e.g., replacing physicians) |
| Consumer wellness (B2C) | Hospital/telehealth diagnostic systems |
| Recovery-informed training adjustment | Multimodal clinical artifact interpretation (ECG, derm photos for diagnosis) |

## Strategic Themes to Track in Research

When comparing research insights to Shifu, look for relevance in:

1. **Conversational AI in health** — dialogue quality, empathy, memory, proactive outreach
2. **Human-AI collaboration** — escalation, handoff, blended threads
3. **Multimodal inputs** — wearables, food photos, chat; vs. clinical imaging/diagnostics
4. **Personalization & state-aware reasoning** — adapting plans to recovery, uncertainty, evolving member state
5. **Adherence & behavior change** — accountability, friction reduction, text-first UX
6. **Evidence-based coaching** — how AI training/grounding relates to clinical evidence
7. **Regulatory boundary** — wellness vs. medical device / diagnostic AI liability
8. **Scalability** — AI handling volume, humans at escalation points

## Competitive Moat Hypotheses

- Text-first coaching reduces friction vs. app-only competitors
- Human escalation in the same thread (not a separate telehealth visit)
- Deep memory across nutrition + training + life context
- Wearable-driven proactive plan changes
- Team of credentialed humans training and supervising the AI Coach
